### Kubernetes and Cloud Native Associate (KCNA)

- Difficulty: Beginner-friendly
- Preparation Time: Around 2–3 weeks
- Notes: This exam covers the fundamentals of Kubernetes and cloud-native concepts. It’s a great starting point if you’re new to the ecosystem.

---

### Kubernetes and Cloud Security Associate (KCSA)

- Difficulty: Low to moderate
- Preparation Time: About 3–4 weeks
- Notes: Focuses on security basics within a Kubernetes context. If you already have a basic understanding of Kubernetes, you may need less time.

---

### Certified Kubernetes Application Developer (CKAD)

- Difficulty: Moderate
- Preparation Time: Around 2–3 months
- Notes: Tailored for developers working on building and deploying containerized applications. It’s generally a bit more accessible for those with a coding background, though the hands-on nature still demands solid practice.

---

### Certified Kubernetes Administrator (CKA)

- Difficulty: Moderate to high
- Preparation Time: Typically 2–3 months
- Notes: Known for its hands-on, performance-based format. Even experienced admins might find it challenging due to time constraints and the breadth of topics (installation, management, troubleshooting, etc.).

---

### Certified Kubernetes Security Specialist (CKS)

- Difficulty: High
- Preparation Time: Approximately 3–4 months
- Notes: This exam dives deep into securing Kubernetes clusters. It covers advanced topics like cluster hardening, vulnerability management, and runtime security. A strong foundation in both Kubernetes and security principles is essential.
